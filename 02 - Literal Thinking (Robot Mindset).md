# 02 - Literal Thinking (Robot Mindset)

## Goal of This Lesson
Train your brain to think in **exact, literal steps**, the way a computer or robot thinks.

This lesson is about removing:
- Guessing
- Assumptions
- “It’s obvious” thinking

And replacing them with:
- Precision
- Clarity
- Step-by-step logic

---

## First Principle 1: Computers Do Not Understand Meaning

A computer does NOT understand:
- Intention
- Context
- Emotion
- Common sense

It only understands **explicit instructions**.

If you do not write a step,
the computer does not know it exists.

---

## Example 1: Human vs Computer Instruction

### Human Instruction
“Sit on the chair.”

### Computer Reality
The computer does not know:
- Which chair?
- Where the chair is?
- How to sit?
- When to stop moving?

Everything must be explained.

---

## First Principle 2: Vague Words Break Logic

Words like:
- Identify
- Select
- Check
- Handle
- Do

Mean NOTHING to a computer.

These words hide real actions.

---

## Example 2: Bad vs Good Thinking

### ❌ Bad
“Check if the door is closed.”

### ✅ Good
1. Look at the door position
2. Compare door position with closed position
3. If positions match, door is closed
4. Otherwise, door is open

---

## First Principle 3: One Step = One Action

Each instruction should do **only one thing**.

### ❌ Bad
“Pick up the phone and open WhatsApp.”

### ✅ Good
1. Pick up the phone
2. Press the power button
3. Unlock the phone
4. Locate WhatsApp icon
5. Tap WhatsApp icon

---

## First Principle 4: Nothing Is Obvious

Humans skip steps.
Computers cannot.

If a step feels obvious to you,
it still must be written.

---

## Example 3: Drinking Water (Robot Thinking)

1. Pick up the glass
2. Move the glass toward mouth
3. Tilt the glass
4. Water enters mouth
5. Stop tilting
6. Place the glass down

---

## First Principle 5: Literal Thinking Creates Control

When instructions are literal:
- Programs behave predictably
- Bugs reduce
- Logic becomes clear

Literal thinking is the foundation of all programming.

---

## Practice Tasks (Do NOT Skip)

### Task 1
Break down **unlocking your phone** into the smallest possible steps.

- Pick up the phone
- Press the power button
- Lockscreen appears
- Enter the password
- Phone gets unlocked

### Task 2
Write robot-level instructions for **sitting on a chair**.

- Move body toward chair
- Place hands on chair seat
- Pull chair backward if needed
- Position chair behind legs
- Bend knees slowly
- Lower body downward
- Stop lowering when body touches chair

### Task 3
Explain **closing a laptop** step by step without skipping actions.

- Place your finger on the trackpad
- Move the cursor to the top left corner of screen
- Click apple icon
- Click Shut down button
- Pop up appears for permission
- Move the cursor to the popup
- Confirm by clicking Shut down again
- Laptop gets Shut down

### Task 4
Describe **turning Wi-Fi on** using state change thinking.

- Move to the lightboard
- Move your finger to the button
- Press the wifi button downwards
- State gets changed from off to on
- Wifi starts

### Task 5
Break down **opening a browser** into literal actions.

- Place your finger on the trackpad.
- Move the cursor to the browser icon
- Press the browser icon once
- Browser gets open

### Task 6
Explain **sending a message on WhatsApp** as if to a robot.

- Pick up the phone
- Press the power button
- Lockscreen appears
- Enter the password
- Phone gets unlocked
- Press the Whatsapp icon
- Whatsapp gets open
- Press the name of the person you want to send a message
- Type the message
- Click send button
- Message gets sent

### Task 7
Describe **scrolling down a webpage** using only physical actions.

- Move your finger tot he trackpad.
- Press the browser icon button.
- Type the website address in the search bar
- Click enter
- Website gets open
- Move two fingers to the trackpad.
- Scroll both fingers at the same time from down to up slowly.
- Webpage gets srolled

### Task 8
Write steps for **checking if a bulb is on** without using the word “check”.

- Move to the lightboard
- Compare the position of bulb switch to when its open
- If both the positions are same bulb is on
- Else its off

### Task 9
Break down **adding one item to a shopping cart** into steps.

- Pick up the phone
- Press the power button
- Lockscreen appears
- Enter the password
- Phone gets unlocked
- Press the shopping app icon
- App gets open
- Click on the item you want to add
- Item page gets open
- Click the Add to Cart button
- Item gets added to the cart

### Task 10
Explain **reducing the volume on your phone** in robot-level detail.

- Pick up the phone
- Press the power button
- Lockscreen appears
- Enter the password
- Phone gets unlocked
- Press the bottom area of volume button
- Each time you press the button, volume gets lowered further.

## Lesson 02 Summary

- Computers need literal instructions
- Vague words hide missing logic
- One step must do one action
- Nothing is obvious to a computer

If you can explain it to a robot,
you can explain it to a computer.
